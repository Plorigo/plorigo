# Plorigo Trademark & Brand Policy

The Plorigo **source code** is open source under the [AGPL-3.0](./LICENSE). The Plorigo
**name, logo, and brand** are **not** part of that license. This policy explains how you may
(and may not) use them. The goal is simple: keep the software free and forkable, while making
sure users can always tell what is the **official** Plorigo product and what is not.

## What this covers

The word mark **"Plorigo"**, the Plorigo logo, and other distinctive brand elements
(collectively, the **"Marks"**).

## You may, without asking

- Use the name "Plorigo" to **truthfully refer to** the project (e.g. "built with Plorigo",
  "a Plorigo plugin", "compatible with Plorigo", blog posts, talks, tutorials, comparisons).
- Run, self-host, modify, and fork the software as the AGPL-3.0 allows.
- Use the name in the **factual** description of a fork (e.g. "this is a fork of Plorigo"), as
  long as you don't imply it is the official project or that we endorse it.

## Please do not, without written permission

- Use the Marks as (or in) the **name of your own product, service, company, or domain** —
  especially a hosted/commercial offering — in a way that suggests it **is** Plorigo or is
  official or endorsed by us.
- **Rebrand a fork** and distribute or sell it as "Plorigo" (or a confusingly similar name).
- Use the Marks in a way that is misleading, disparaging, or that implies partnership,
  sponsorship, or endorsement that doesn't exist.
- Modify the logo or use it as your own brand identity.

If you distribute a modified version, please give it **your own name** and make clear it is not
the official Plorigo distribution.

## Why this exists

AGPL keeps the code open — anyone can self-host or fork it. But trademarks protect **users**:
they ensure that when someone downloads "Plorigo" or signs up for "Plorigo Cloud", they're
getting the real thing. This is standard practice for open-source projects (the Linux, Python,
and many other communities protect their names the same way) and is fully compatible with the
software being free and open.
